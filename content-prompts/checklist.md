# Template: Action Checklist (checklists)
- Open with a 50-word framing intro: what the checklist covers and the outcome the reader achieves by completing it.
- Use imperative, action-oriented language throughout. Each checklist item = one concrete action.
- No comparison_table required.

## Required sections (in order)
1. **What This Covers** — Brief definition of the topic, why this checklist matters, and who it is for (kind: definition_box).
2. **Pre- [Phase]** — Setup, preparation, or baseline actions (kind: list, imperative items, rationale in parentheses).
3. **During [Phase]** — In-session or active-implementation actions (kind: list).
4. **Post- [Phase]** — Recovery, review, or follow-up actions (kind: list).
5. **Common Mistakes to Avoid** — 3-5 pitfalls written as "Don't..." items (kind: list).
6. **FAQs** — Minimum 3. Focus on implementation edge cases: frequency, adaptations for beginners, what to do when X goes wrong.

## Structure rules
- Each list item must be a single imperative sentence followed by a brief rationale in parentheses.
  Example: "Set a consistent wake time 7 days a week (anchors your circadian rhythm regardless of sleep duration)."
- Lifecycle phases must be labelled to match the topic. For sleep: Pre-Sleep / During Sleep Window / Morning. For race prep: Pre-Race Week / Race Day / Post-Race. Adapt accordingly.
- Mandatory verdict block with exactly 3 bullets:
  1) Best for: ...
  2) Avoid if: ...
  3) Bottom line: ...
- Mandatory CTA context: ring + $1/mo newsletter + free protocol PDF.
- All internal links MUST use markdown format: `[descriptive anchor text](/template/slug)`. Never use bare paths like `/guides/slug` without an anchor.
